What's doing the work

  • Tier headers in tracked gold caps with a hairline fade — gives the page its almanac rhythm. Five tiers: Luminaries · Personal · Social · Outer · Points.
  • Three-column row: glyph (fixed 24pt column) · sign+planet (sign in Fraunces italic gold, planet name whispered in serif italic cream-soft) · house (tracked caps with a gold ordinal).
  • Ship path: brain emits richer markdown from _chart_ready_headline() — tier dividers + per-line house data. Zero new event types needed. The existing Bubble.tsx markdown renderer handles it via custom heading + bullet styles.
  • Reading order stays top-to-bottom — luminaries first, what most users came for.

What's doing the work

  • The wheel — 268pt SVG medallion. 12 house spokes + Asc/Desc/MC/IC compass labels + zodiac glyphs around the rim + planet glyphs placed at their house mid-angles. Center rosette holds the eye.
  • Asc on the left (astrology convention — 9 o'clock). Houses count counter-clockwise from there. The user's actual Ascendant rotates the zodiac ring; sample shows Scorpio rising.
  • Below the wheel: same typographic page as Direction A (abbreviated here for layout). The wheel is a visual anchor; the list is the legible truth.
  • Ship path: brain emits a new chart_wheel event (or piggybacks on media) with the planet/sign/house data; iOS draws the wheel SVG with react-native-svg. Brain also emits the markdown typography. ~1 new component (ChartWheel.tsx) + a few lines in _chart_ready_headline().
  • Tradeoff: more code (~150 lines) and the wheel adds visual weight that competes with Layla's voice. But the moment of the chart arriving is THE moment of the app — a centerpiece earns its keep here.

recommendation

Ship Direction A first. Zero new event types, zero new components — just richer markdown from _chart_ready_headline(). The typographic page already feels like a gift unfolded; the wheel is the next move once Direction A lands and you want to crown it.
